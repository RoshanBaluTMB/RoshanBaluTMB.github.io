01 · About

Engineering at the intersection of vision, robotics, and automation

I'm an Automation Systems Engineer at Sims Lifecycle Services in West Chicago, where I work with external automation vendors and cross-functional teams to build robotic workcells and integrate MES platforms, OEE analytics, and quality testing metrics. Previously, as Mechatronics Engineer there, I led the development of industrial machine vision systems for QA — combining GigE cameras, Keyence sensors, and custom YOLOv8 models with Fanuc and Mitsubishi robotics to automate high-speed inspection and component handling.

My background spans a M.S. in Mechatronics and Robotics from NYU, where I researched autonomous UAV charging systems at the Agile Robotics and Perception Lab, and earlier work in India designing SCADA-integrated autoloader and vision systems that lifted production by 35%. I care about systems that ship: reliable CI/CD, clean CAD/PLM data management, and automation that measurably reduces operator strain. I also take physical AI hardware from ideation through DFM myself — everything from editing G-code on FDM printers to setting up and maintaining a Formlabs SLA printer from scratch — to prototype, build, and repair robot hardware end-to-end.
